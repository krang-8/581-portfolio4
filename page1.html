CPSC 581: Project 1 (The HackRage Button)

Overall Description

In a world where security threats on the internet are rapid and ever evolving one’s information needs to be protected. A good password can be the one thing between losing it all. And when you get hacked it can be an incredibly frustrating time that can make anyone mad.

This random password generator button allows the user to create passwords for use on any platform at the same time being an outlet of anger. The platform allows users to customize the password outcome while making the main interaction the button that generated the resulting password. Our team wanted to create simplistic utility for a real-life problem that many people face for project 1 in the Human-Computer Interactions II course at the University of Calgary.

Initial Sketches

Password Generator    

A password generator program based on the idea of generating STRONG passwords for users who lack the awareness to make their own to keep users safe from online attacks.

This idea was chosen due to it's ability of being malleable enough to make it novel and special, we were able to think of a variety of detailed ideas on how to make it unique and interactive while keeping it's simplicity.

Synchronized Chess Timer

A program where two devices become dependent buttons which rely on each other and switch the timer to the opponent's turn upon clicking on the screen. Two issues for this, I do not play Chess, and lack of time to implement.

This idea was not chosen due to my lack of knowledge regarding Chess and being hard to implement on short notice due to the synchronization efforts and obtaining a physical LED timer to connect

Music Note Clicker

A program where different buttons indicate different musical notes, allowing people with learning difficulties to easily recognize different musical notes with a visual and audio feedback.

This idea was not chosen due to lack of interest from the team.

Ringtone Clicker

A simple program where clicking on the screen plays a random ringtone! Ever need to escape from an awkward situation? Click the button and dart away!

This idea was not chosen as while it does have a useful use case scenario, the password generator idea was more adaptable and more interesting for the group to implement.

Cookie Clicker

A game based around repeatedly clicking on a cookie at the center of the screen, which each press you get a $ amount based on the amount of times you clicked in a short span of time. The goal is to reach as large of a $ amount as possible.

We were not able to think of many ways to make it unique as the video game "Cookie Clicker" already exists with the same purpose and features. In addition, it lacked utility. Hence, it was eliminated from our selection.

Higher or Lower!

A minigame where you are shown images of two different, unique objects and you have to guess which has a higher/lower average price.

We were not able to think of many ways to make it unique as the video game "Higher or Lower" already exists with the same purpose and features. Hence, it was eliminated. In addition, it lacked utility.

Simon Says Minigame

A minigame based on the fun game "Simon Says". The goal of this game is to hit the shapes that previously glowed or moved in the same exact order.

This idea was not chosen due to it's lack of malleability to making it novel, in addition, it lacked utility.

Guess The Capital

A minigame where you are prompted with a country with the goal of being able to correctly guess the capital city of said country.

This idea was not chosen as we chose to focus on something with more utility.

Fragrance Notes Visualizer

A program to help visualize different top/middle/bottom fragrance notes of random fragrances sourced from fragrantica.com

This idea was not chosen due to the fact it would require us to maintain a database of fragrances and their separate notes.

Hangman Minigame

A minigame based on "Hangman". A fun way to kill time, the goal of the game is to guess the word using the letters provided and to fill in the blanks before you run out of attempts!

This idea was not chosen due to it promoting violence as we feel someone being hanged is very violent in the context of the classroom.

Word Of The Day!

A program which provides a random word and it's definition with the ability to cycle through different words by clicking on the "Next Word" button.

This idea was not chosen due to it's lack of novelty and over-simplicity.

Detailed Sketches

Checkboxes

This design is based on the idea of using check boxes to "tick" criteria for the password to be generator.

While this idea is a good base, we chose to implement further details to build on it and make it more novel in the process.

Slider Design

This design is based on the idea of a simple slider to determine the length and a button to generate the password. This was made as an iteration of the previous.

This idea was not chosen due to it's lack of novelty and over-simplicity.

The No-Control Prototype

This design is based on the idea of having no control at all and relying on the algorithm to randomly generate a password for the user.

This idea was not chosen because we wanted to user to have more interactivity and be more in control

Dials and Knobs!

This design was going back to the drawing board and iterating on the original idea, the thought process behind this design is to give the user more control again while doing it in a more interactive/cool way.

This idea was not chosen as we found knobs and dials to be a problematic tool to use for adjusting the criteria.

Version 5

This design is an iteration on previous designs, it takes the idea of the check boxes and the slider and tries to incorporate both into a single design.


The One Button

This design is based on simplicity, a simple "generate" button which would then be replaced with the generated password prompt.

This idea was not chosen due to it's lack of novelty and over-simplicity.

The Phraser!

This design was based on the idea of entering a phrase into a text box and allowing the algorithm to incorporate that phrase into the randomly generated password.

This idea was not chosen due to it's lack of novelty and over-simplicity.

Lamps R Buttons 2

This design was an iteration on previous designs, it brings back control to the user but in a cool and novel way! LED lights! The idea was to have LED lights control the criteria of which characters are allowed in the generated password.

This idea was not chosen due to the possible poor user experience upon implementation.

Shape Generator

This design was based on the idea of abstracting the controls while still allowing more experienced users to generate custom-tailored passwords. The idea was to have each shape correspond to a type of character, and upon clicking each shape it would generate one instance of that type of character, so the user would click a bunch of symbols in a minigame and end up with a randomly generated password.


Vertical Sliders

This design builds on the slider variant of our program, instead of just one slider to control the length of the password, why not give a slider to control the quantity of each type of character present in said password (such as digits, letters, symbols, whitespaces etc)
 

Sketch 11

A password generator that allows a user to increase the length of the password by repeatedly clicking the generate password button and customize the password by checking off the options to include upper letters, numbers, and special characters. So, when an a user is angry, button pressing will be a great outlet for emotions while solving the issue with a new password!

The Final Design: Sketch 11

The idea is with more interaction with the button the stronger and more complex the password becomes, thus providing a great first line of defense against unauthorized access. The stronger the password, the more protected one’s computer will be from malicious entities. We ended up choosing this because the idea was a unique spin on the traditional password generator that provided the same real-life utility at the same time as being a creative use of button interaction. For the technical components, the project with constructed with best practice use of java script, css, and html.

I contributed to the base code by writing an initial version of the password generator with a basic UI interface in addition to participating with the group by sketching ideas and brainstorming over them to finalize our final design: Sketch 11.
